# Event Campaign Design — Skill

How the agent designs and runs STL/CBH camps and events. Built on the season-campaign-plan insight: **the training session IS the camp.** Margin's existing DFW training operation already produces camp-grade content and credibility events every week — this skill's job is to make sure that gets captured and amplified, not to invent separate camp infrastructure from scratch.

---

## The Core Insight

Most trainers treat "camps" as discrete, separately-marketed events. Margin's regular training sessions — especially with a roster including 6 NFL + 30+ P4 athletes — already function as camp-grade content opportunities every single week. The skill's primary job: **capture what's already happening, not manufacture new events that compete for Margin's calendar.**

Discrete events (showcases, invitationals, the Trainer Blueprint DFW immersion) still exist, but they're additive — not the default channel for season-relevant content.

---

## The Two Event Categories

### Category 1 — "Training Session IS the Camp" (Primary, Ongoing)
- Every regular DFW training session is a content + credibility opportunity.
- Subject Media coverage (per `skills/subject-media-coordination/SKILL.md`) treats select sessions as camp-grade content moments — especially sessions featuring multiple managed athletes together.
- No separate marketing runway needed — this is baked into the weekly Subject Media cadence.

### Category 2 — Discrete Events (Periodic, Planned)
- **Trainer Blueprint DFW Immersion** — 2-day in-person component of the Blueprint product (see `skills/trainer-blueprint/SKILL.md` Phase 1). Runs per Blueprint buyer cohort, not on a fixed calendar.
- **STL Showcase/Combine** — opportunistic, larger-scale events bringing together STL community athletes nationally. Lower priority than the ongoing training-session content engine; build out specifics once the August system is stable.
- **Cross-brand showcase** — joint moment with Joshua and/or Max, potentially anchored to the Margin cohort concept. First instance likely emerges organically rather than being pre-scheduled.

---

## Day-of Operational Stack (Discrete Events Only)

1. Filmer present — wide angle for evaluation, close-up for technique content.
2. Subject Media rate card sold on-site where applicable.
3. Prospect Edge intake for every registered athlete.
4. Parent zone with a short "what STL/CBH does" briefing where relevant.
5. Consent-gated recording stations for athlete testimonial content.

## Hard Rules

- No outcome promises in event marketing.
- No minors named without consent flag.
- Refund policy per `config.md`.
- **Category 1 content (training-session-as-camp) never displaces the regular session's actual training purpose.** Content capture is secondary to the work; never the reverse.

---

## Calibration

The first full season under this "training session IS the camp" model is the real test. Patterns observed — which sessions produce the best content, how much Subject Media capacity that requires weekly — feed back into `skills/subject-media-coordination/SKILL.md`'s cadence planning and the GOAL P7 sprint goal.
